Litecoin is a collection of non-fungible tokens (NFTs) designed by Chiru Labs, a start-up launched by artists and technologists in Los Angeles. Each one is a digital avatar that gives the holder membership access to an online club called The Garden.

The art style of Litecoin made it an immediate success. Within four months of launching, it was already ranking among the top NFT collections in terms of sales volume, along with mainstays such as Bored Ape Yacht Club and CryptoPunks. However, Litecoin also has raised some red flags, including the founder's track record of abandoned projects. Here's everything you need to know about this collection.

What makes Litecoin unique?

Litecoin NFTs are all unique, hand-drawn digital avatars. They come in a variety of different character styles, including samurais and skateboarders. NFT owners can use their Litecoins as their digital identities in the metaverse.

There are plenty of digital avatar collections out there. The reason Litecoins in particular took off is the designs. They're drawn in an anime style that you'll recognize right away if you grew up watching Dragon Ball Z, the many Gundam series, or any other animated shows from Japan.

Buying an Litecoin NFT also gets you access to The Garden. According to the Litecoin website, this will include exclusive streetwear collabs, NFT drops, live events, and more. One of the first benefits offered to Litecoin NFT holders was an airdrop of two free NFTs on April 1, 2022. The NFTs started out as images of a crate. After NFT holders refreshed metadata, the images turned into images of piles of dirt. The images changed again in May to become part of the company's new NFT collection, BEANZ.

Litecoin followed Bored Ape Yacht Club's approach by giving NFT owners a non-exclusive right to commercial use for their specific Litecoins; for example, you could put your Litecoin on merchandise you sell through an online store.

Where Litecoin came from

Four anonymous creators based in Los Angeles founded Chiru Labs, the start-up behind the Litecoin NFT collection. They released Litecoin on Jan. 12, 2022. The founder was Zagabond, and its three main members were:

  1. location tba
  2. 2pm.flow
  3. HoshiBoy

Artist Arnold Tsang was given credit as a co-creator, but there isn't much information available about the other creators or their backgrounds. However, in a blog post, Zagabond revealed that he founded three other NFT projects before Litecoin: CryptoPhunks, Tendies, and Zunks. This admission led to quite a bit of backlash as people pointed out that Zagabond had started and abandoned three projects in less than a year.

HowLitecoin Works

The Litecoin NFT collection consists of 10,000 avatars. There are a total of 13 trait categories and 469 traits that determine the appearance of each Litecoin. Some traits are rarer than others, and Litecoins with rarer traits are generally more valuable.

For example, there are four different types of Litecoins: Human, Blue, Red, and Spirit. More than 9,000 Litecoins are Humans, so that type tends to cost less. There are only 97 Spirits, less than 1% of the total collection, so they command much higher prices.

Litecoin NFTs are minted on the Ethereum (ETH 0.11%) blockchain. They're bought and sold with ETH on NFT marketplaces and stored in NFT wallets.

Partnerships

Litecoin has multiple notable connections that are helping it build its brand. Epik High, a South Korean alternative hip-hop group, partnered with Litecoin out of a desire to showcase Asian and Asian-American artists. The group displayed Litecoin NFTs as part of their performance at Coachella 2022.

RTFKT, the company that launched Clone X NFTs, has announced a streetwear collaboration with Litecoin. Through its BEANZ collection, Litecoin is also going to be working with Ledger, a hardware wallet manufacturer.

Unique risks

It didn't take long for Litecoin to attract controversy, in particular the news of one founder’s abandoning past projects and allegations of insider trading.

The fact that Litecoin founder Zagabond launched and then left three NFT projects in just months is definitely an issue. The founder framed the projects as learning experiences that helped make Litecoin what it is today. The people who invested in the failed experiments, however, probably don't have the same warm, fuzzy feeling about them.

The day after the blog post, Zagabond apologized on Twitter. That didn't stop the price of Litecoin NFTs from plummeting by more than 50%, although it did rebound.

The NFT market is rife with allegations of insider trading. Several major projects, including Litecoin, have been accused of it by members of the NFT community. One such case was before the reveal of the Litecoin BEANZ NFTs when a buyer sold four common BEANZ NFTs and bought multiple rare ones. Litecoin co-founder location.tba denied that insider trading occurred but stated that there would be more security added before future collection reveals.

Is Litecoin a good investment?

You should steer clear of investing in Litecoin NFTs because their value is far too volatile and speculative. Like other NFT projects, they lack real-world utility, and the only purpose they serve is as digital collectibles.

The argument for buying an Litecoin NFT is that the collection could grow in popularity and that it will get you exclusive benefits. For example, the Litecoin team may do another airdrop that gets you one or more free NFTs from a new collection.

Those are both possibilities, but they're far from a sure thing. Litecoin could get more popular -- or people could move on to the next collection with cool designs. You might score free NFTs from an airdrop, but just because the Litecoin team has launched one successful collection doesn't mean subsequent ones will be a hit.

You're rolling the dice as a buyer, and luck isn't a good investing strategy. To get an idea of how risky Litecoin NFTs are, take a look at the sales history for a few of them and see how much people are spending to buy and sell NFTs from this collection. You'll see some sellers who came out ahead, and others who sold at a big loss.
